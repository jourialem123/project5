Apples

Apples are a sweet, red and crunchy fruit that grows on trees.Apple trees are cultivated worldwide and are the most widely grown species in the genus Malus.

The tree originated in Central Asia, where its wild ancestor, Malus sieversii, is still found. Apples have been grown for thousands of years in Eurasia and were introduced to North America by European colonists. Apples have religious and mythological significance in many cultures, including Norse, Greek, and European Christian tradition.
There are more than 7,500 cultivars of apples. Different cultivars are bred for various tastes and uses, including cooking, eating raw, and apple juice production.

Apples have many benefits which includes:
  1. Stabilizes blood sugar
  2. Lowers cholesterol
  3. Reduces blood pressure
  4. Eases inflammation
  5. Satisfies hunger longer
